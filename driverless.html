Shared technology

The same core, two very different tracks

The technologies at the heart of Hermes derive directly from the driverless projects developed with MMR: the know-how built on the circuit now lives inside our hospital robots.

LiDAR perception

The same automotive-grade Hesai OT128 sensor used on the driverless car. Thanks to consolidated commercial agreements with the manufacturer, we bring race-validated hardware into healthcare at a competitive cost.

SLAM & localization

Real-time mapping and self-localization algorithms born to keep a car on the racing line — now letting Hermes know exactly where it is inside a hospital, with no GPS available indoors.

Autonomous motion

Sensor-fusion, obstacle detection and safe trajectory planning in dynamic environments. On track it dodges cones at speed; in the ward it safely avoids beds, equipment and people.

Olympus builds its technological path side by side with MMR – MoRe Modena Racing, developing autonomous vehicles and advanced environmental-perception systems within the Formula SAE driverless project.

First robots

The first autonomous logistics robots are built thanks to this partnership, progressively refining performance, reliability and safety by transferring automotive know-how to industrial use.

Into industry

Today — healthcare

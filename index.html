The problem

Medication logistics is still manual

In hospitals, drug distribution still relies almost entirely on human effort: variable timing, fragmented traceability and a high risk of clinical error.

∿

Staff pulled away from care

A significant share of doctors' and nurses' time is absorbed by transport and repetitive logistics tasks.

⚠

Administration errors

The heavy manual component increases the probability of errors in managing and delivering drugs.

⛓

Fragmented traceability

Prescriptions, pharmacy and logistics often live on separate, non-integrated systems.

⏱

Non-standard timing

Delivery times vary with workload and the availability of operators.

The solution

One ecosystem, from pharmacy to patient

Hermes isn't just a robot: it's an integrated platform combining automated storage, autonomous robotics and clinical software into a single, continuous, traceable flow.

01 · Storage

Modula Next cabinets

High-density vertical storage in the central pharmacy: guided picking, real-time stock management and every drug uniquely identified.

02 · Transport

Hermes autonomous robot

LiDAR + SLAM navigation, omnidirectional movement and modular drawers — standard, refrigerated and access-controlled — digitally assigned to each individual patient.

03 · Coordination

Clinical software

Three layers — clinician app, robot fleet and Modula integration — orchestrate requests, administrations and end-to-end traceability on a single platform.

Features

Built for the real ward

Every feature is born from a concrete operational need of healthcare facilities.

Follow-Me mode

The robot follows the clinician through corridors via Bluetooth Low Energy — like a shadow assistant — keeping safe distance from other robots.

Elevator control

An encrypted short-range radio pulse simulates a button press: no invasive modifications to the existing infrastructure.

Refrigerated drawers

Temperature-controlled modules for heat-sensitive drugs and reinforced compartments for high-risk substances.

Selective badge unlock

The clinician's NFC/RFID badge opens only that patient's specific drawer, automatically logging the time in the clinical record.

Emergency call

On-demand dispatch: in case of need, the clinician summons the nearest available robot straight from their smartphone.

End-to-end traceability

Every movement of the drug is recorded: where it is, who picked it up and which patient it's destined for.

How it works

A day with Hermes

From loading in the pharmacy to returning for sanitization, the entire cycle is automated and supervised.

1

Preparation

The software gathers ward requests and generates the loading list. The pharmacist fills the drawers, guided by Modula codes.

2

Transit

The robot sets off autonomously, navigates with LiDAR and SLAM and avoids dynamic obstacles. It calls elevators via radio.

3

Ward round

It locks onto the clinician in Follow-Me mode and waits outside rooms until authorized through the app.

4

Administration

The clinician taps their badge: only the patient's drawer unlocks. The therapy is checked off and recorded.

5

Return

Once the round is done, the robot heads back to the pharmacy for sanitization and recharging at the docking station.
